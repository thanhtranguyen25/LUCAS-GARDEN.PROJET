About Me

I am a botany and landscape design student from Cape Town, currently pursuing my Master's degree in Conservation Botany with a focus on drought-resistant landscaping at the University of Cape Town. My research investigates how indigenous South African plants can be adapted to Mediterranean climates, providing sustainable and beautiful landscaping solutions for water-scarce regions.

Having grown up in a region that regularly experiences drought, I developed an early interest in xeriscaping principles and water conservation. The unique fynbos biome of the Western Cape has particularly influenced my approach to design, inspiring me to create gardens that balance ecological functionality with aesthetic appeal.

At Lucas Gardens, I am exploring the similarities between South African and Mediterranean plant communities, learning advanced irrigation techniques, and developing planting schemes that can thrive with minimal water input while maintaining visual interest throughout the seasons.
