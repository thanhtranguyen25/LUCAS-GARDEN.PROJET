Our Lawn Care Services

We offer a comprehensive range of lawn care services to keep your grass healthy, vibrant, and beautiful throughout the year.

Professional Mowing

Regular mowing at the optimal height for your grass type to promote healthy growth and a neat appearance. Our experts use sharp, well-maintained equipment to ensure precise cuts without damaging the grass.

Aeration

Core aeration to reduce soil compaction, allowing air, water, and nutrients to penetrate deeper into the soil. This promotes stronger root development and healthier grass growth.

Overseeding

Strategic seeding to fill in thin areas, introduce improved grass varieties, and increase density. This helps crowd out weeds and create a more uniform, lush appearance.

Fertilization

Custom fertilization programs that provide essential nutrients at the right time for your specific grass type and soil conditions. Our balanced approach promotes healthy growth without harmful runoff.

Weed Control

Targeted pre-emergent and post-emergent weed control treatments to manage unwanted plants while protecting your grass. We use selective products for specific weed problems when necessary.

Pest Management

Monitoring and treatment for common lawn pests such as grubs, chinch bugs, and armyworms. We employ integrated pest management strategies that minimize chemical use while effectively protecting your lawn.

Disease Control

Prevention and treatment of common lawn diseases such as brown patch, dollar spot, and rust. Our approach includes cultural practices and targeted fungicide applications when necessary.

Irrigation Management

Expert advice on watering practices and schedules to maintain optimal soil moisture. We can also work with your irrigation system to ensure efficient coverage and prevent water waste.

Dethatching

Removal of excessive thatch (dead grass) that can block water, nutrients, and air from reaching the soil. This improves lawn health and appearance while reducing disease susceptibility.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should my lawn be mowed?

The ideal mowing frequency depends on your grass type, growth rate, and season. As a general rule, most lawns should be mowed when they reach about one-third taller than their optimal height. During active growing seasons, this typically means weekly mowing, while during slower growth periods, mowing can be less frequent. Our experts will recommend the optimal schedule for your specific lawn.

When is the best time to fertilize my lawn?

The optimal fertilization timing depends on your grass type and climate. Cool-season grasses (like fescue) typically benefit from heavier fertilization in fall and lighter applications in spring. Warm-season grasses (like Bermuda) often need more fertilizer in late spring and summer. Our lawn care programs include properly timed fertilization tailored to your specific grass type and local conditions.

How do I know if my lawn needs aeration?

Signs that your lawn may need aeration include water pooling on the surface, difficulty pushing a screwdriver into the soil, thinning grass, increased weed pressure, and heavy foot traffic areas. Most lawns benefit from annual aeration, while heavily used or clay-soil lawns may need it twice yearly. Our lawn assessment will determine if aeration would benefit your lawn.

Do you offer organic lawn care options?

Yes, we offer organic and hybrid lawn care programs that minimize synthetic chemical use while maintaining lawn health and appearance. These programs focus on building soil health through natural amendments, proper cultural practices, and selective use of organic products. While these programs may take longer to achieve full results compared to conventional programs, they provide excellent long-term benefits for your lawn and the environment.
